Is chronic tonsillitis –
a sentence?

Almost everyone has faced sore throat and tonsillitis at some moment over a lifetime. While the diagnosis of recurrent tonsillitis will suppose something terrible for many. What is this disease and how to treat it?

TONSILLITIS is an acute or chronic inflammation of the palatine tonsils – formations on the lateral surfaces deep in the pharynx, similar to almonds. The tonsils are pierced with special slits – lacunae. Lacunae are traps for microbes and viruses, as well as a kind of "polygon" where the immune system learns to fight these microbes and viruses. They are the tonsils that first meet pathogens from the external environment and provide protection for our body.  ACUTE TONSILLITIS or ANGINA is an acute infectious disease with a predominant lesion of the tonsils. At the same time, both the tonsils themselves and the whole organism suffer. In most cases, acute tonsillitis is caused by viruses, but especially dangerous are bacteria: streptococci or staphylococci. It is manifested by severe tonsillitis, inflammation and an increase in the size of palatine tonsils, high fever. The appearance of purulent deposits is possible. After suffering from tonsillitis, the immune system decreases and it becomes more difficult for the body to resist infections.

The presence of chronic tonsillitis means that the tonsils cannot cope with their protective function and fail to ensure the functioning of local and general immunity in full.

FREQUENT TONSILLITIS can also lead to serious complications:

paratonsillar abscess
acute rheumatic fever
kidney damage (glomerulonephritis)
joint inflammation (arthritis)
frequent colds

Therefore, it is very important not only to get rid of pathogens, but also to restore the work of the tonsils.

For the treatment of recurrent tonsillitis, various groups of drugs are being widely used, aimed at both eliminating the infection (antibiotics, antiseptics) and restoring the protective function of the body (immunomodulators, vitamins, natural complex preparations).

Do tonsils have to
be removed?

Modern trends in the treatment of chronic tonsillitis in children are based on the most careful attitude to the tonsils as an immunocompetent organ (the organ responsible for immunity), the removal of which is justified only in cases of proven functional unfitness and hypertrophy of the Waldeer-Pirogov pharyngeal lymphoid tissue ring.

Doctors prefer conservative therapy, which is carried out in three directions:

- elimination of the inflammatory process in the palatine tonsils;
- improvement of drainage function of lacunae of the palatine tonsils;
- normalization of local and general immunity.

To improve the drainage function of the lacunae of the Palatine tonsils, suction of purulent contents, rinsing with antiseptic solutions, massage of the tonsils, application of various preparations on their surface are used. To increase local and general immunity, drugs with an immunomodulatory effect are used.

The course of conservative therapy is carried out every 6 months. If there is no positive result after 2-3 courses, doctors shall decide as for surgical treatment (tonsillectomy)1.

If you suffer from tonsillitis, it is important that your throat is sufficiently hydrated.

Opinions differ, whichever is better: cold or warm drinks? Cold drinks or ice cream can relieve pain well, but at the same time blood flow to inflamed areas worsens, which in turn can slow down healing and recovery. Therefore, most rely on warm tea.

Also, a warm scarf around the neck can improve the condition. Exposure of heat improves blood flow and accelerates the healing process.

Since eating is often hampered by irritation and inflammation in the throat, soft or liquid foods, such as porridge or soup, are particularly suitable for supplying the body with sufficient nutrients during infection. Chicken broth is especially useful. Honey also usually helps with tonsillitis. However, it should be noted that it should not be dissolved in too hot tea.

Rinses with a slightly warm decoction of herbs (calendula, chamomile, sage) are effective.
